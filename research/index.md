The Frasch Lab investigates maternal-fetal physiology and neuroscience across several interconnected areas. We study how chronic maternal stress during pregnancy manifests in heartbeat patterns, develop non-invasive monitoring technologies, and build computational models to advance perinatal medicine.

## Brain Research

<div class="card-grid">

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/brain/stress/' | relative_url }}">Prenatal Stress</a></h3>
<p>Investigating how chronic maternal stress affects brain development in utero. Stressed fetuses entrain their heartbeat to the maternal heartbeat, but non-stressed fetuses do not.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/brain/neuroinflammation/' | relative_url }}">Neuroinflammation</a></h3>
<p>The cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway, microglial inflammation models, and the brain-gut connection in utero.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/brain/ischemia/' | relative_url }}">Brain Ischemia</a></h3>
<p>Early and reliable detection of fetal brain injury remains an unsolved challenge. We develop HRV-based approaches to solve it.</p>
</div>

</div>

## Biosensors

<div class="card-grid">

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/biosensors/ecg/' | relative_url }}">ECG</a></h3>
<p>Non-invasive fetal ECG monitoring using FDA-approved transabdominal monitors and multidimensional HRV analyses.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/biosensors/eeg/' | relative_url }}">EEG</a></h3>
<p>Fetal EEG monitoring during labor to predict severe acidemia and prevent brain injury.</p>
</div>

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/biosensors/nerve-stimulation/' | relative_url }}">Nerve Stimulation</a></h3>
<p>Vagus nerve stimulation to combat sepsis.</p>
</div>

</div>

## minAction.net

<div class="card-grid">

<div class="card">
<h3><a href="{{ '/research/minaction/' | relative_url }}">minAction.net</a></h3>
<p>A search for vertically organizing principles in physiology — moving from studying causality within specific scales to finding principles that are invariant across all scales, inspired by the Principle of Least Action in physics.</p>
</div>

</div>
